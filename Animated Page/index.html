A Process Scheduler schedules different processes to be assigned to the CPU based on particular scheduling algorithms. There are four(4) process scheduling algorithms included on this site. These algorithms are either non-preemptive or preemptive. Non-preemptive algorithms are designed so that once a process enters the running state, it cannot be preempted until it completes its allotted time, whereas the preemptive scheduling is based on priority where a scheduler may preempt a low priority running process anytime when a high priority process enters into a ready state. FCFS and SJN are both preemptive algorithms while SRT and Round Robin are both non-preemptive algorithms.
